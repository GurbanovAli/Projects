Scrum - “scramble” The word scrum

Scrum - “scramble” The word scrum (“scramble”) is taken from rugby and refers to a team game method that allows you to take the ball and lead it further across the field, and this requires

  1. coherence
  2. unity of intent
  3. a clear understanding of the goal

Scrum Method Mechanism

Step 1. Choose a product owner

Step 2. Choose a team

Conditions for choosing a team

Step 3. Choose a scrum - master (coordinator)

Scrum - master is a person who monitors the progress of the project

  • provides short meetings (sprints)
  • helps the team remove obstacles

Step 4. Create a product backlog.

Algorithm for compiling a backlog (action program):

  1. make a list of absolutely all the requirements (tasks) presented to the project (product);
  2. arrange them (requirements, tasks) according to their priority;
  3. provide feedback to the product owner.

Step 5. Refine and evaluate the backlog of the product

Step 6. Planning a sprint. Sprint is a scrum meeting.

Step 7. Daily scrum

During the sprint cycle, every day the scrum team meets for no more than 15 minutes.

Step 8. Sprint review

A sprint review is an open meeting where the team demonstrates to what extent they managed to move to the “Done” column during the sprint.

Step 9. Retrospective meeting

A retrospective meeting occurs after a review of the outcome. The SCRUM team gathers, discusses and documents.
